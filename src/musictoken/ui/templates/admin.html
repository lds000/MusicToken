🛠 Hardware tips — filament, glue & finishing

Filament per genre

PolyTerra Matte PLA is the recommended default — saturated color, hides layer lines on a thin disc, prints clean at 0.12 mm. Avoid metallic / silk / glitter / carbon-fill / stainless-fill: metal flake attenuates NFC.

Base coin

  • Standard NTAG215 25 mm PVC coin (~1 mm thick). Verified: Amazon ASIN B09L7LBYZ3.
  • Total stack with the 0.8 mm top plate ≈ 1.8 mm — well within read range for any PN532/RC522.
  • You don't program the chip. MusicToken reads the factory hardware UID; blank coins out of the bag are fine.

Print settings

  • Layer height 0.12 mm, infill 100%.
  • No supports, no brim. Bottom face is the bond face.

PEI textured plate gives the bond face a slight stipple — that's good, more grip for 3M 468MP. Skip M600 filament swaps; paint-fill is faster and looks identical. Batch 12–25 chips per plate, swap filament between genres.

Glue (best → fastest)

  1. 3M 468MP transfer tape, 25 mm round die-cut — peel, stick, press 5 s. Best for batches. Search Aliexpress: "25mm 3M 468MP round".
  2. Loctite Super Glue Liquid Universal — thin ring around the edge only. Don't pool in the center (CA fogs).
  3. Avoid: hot glue, epoxy, Gorilla Original (foams).

Prep + paint-fill

  • Light 400-grit pass on both mating faces, wipe with 91%+ IPA. Roughly doubles bond strength.
  • For paint-fill: brush acrylic over the top, wait ~30 s, wipe with damp microfiber. Paint stays in the engraved letters.
  • White or silver paint on dark filaments; black paint on light.
